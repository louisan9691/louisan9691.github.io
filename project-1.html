CASE STUDY

GAMIFIED BIOLOGY SCORM


MY ROLE

SCORM Designer

SCORM Developer


CLIENT

Monash College


Year

2019

This project was undertaken to develop two technology enhanced visual gamified (TEVGIS) resources to support student learning and understanding of cell membrane structure and function, and enzyme activity. The two resources were developed, created and tested as “in class” activities for foundation year Biology Unit 1 students. The resources are available online to support students learning in class as well as allow students to learn at their own pace. As the name suggests, TEVGIS resources are digital, gamified activities, which use visual representations and animations and provide immediate feedback to enhance student’s engagement and learning. Introduction of the TEVGIS clearly outlines the purpose and expected time required for completing the activities. Students are navigated and prompted through the activities with plenty of feedback provided, allowed an unlimited number of attempts and awarded trophies for their efforts. Working on the activity allowed students to think critically, to work collaboratively, engage in ‘hands on’ activity and develop problem solving and communication skills. These technology enhanced activities supported learners to revise key concepts in a way that is interactive and provided them with hints and feedback about their understanding as they engaged in self-study to possibly aid with long-term retention.
